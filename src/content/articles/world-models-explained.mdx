## The result

Recent world-model systems achieve strong performance on simulated control and video prediction benchmarks by learning latent dynamics rather than pixel-level generation.

## The core idea

A world model compresses observations into states, predicts transitions, and optionally plans actions by rolling forward in latent space. The objective is consistency of future representations, not necessarily photorealistic renders.

## Why it matters

Agents that can simulate futures internally may generalize better under distribution shift and use compute more efficiently at inference than brute-force search over language tokens alone.

## Limitations

Long-horizon compounding error, ambiguous evaluation metrics, and unclear scaling laws relative to autoregressive LLMs remain open problems.

## My take

World models are best understood as complementary infrastructure: valuable where interaction and physics matter, insufficient alone for open-ended knowledge work without language interfaces.
