## The result

World models learn to predict how an environment evolves — often in a compressed latent space — so an agent can plan actions without rolling out full physics in pixel space.

## The core idea

Separate **representation**, **dynamics**, and **policy**:

- Encode observations.
- Predict next latents (or energies) conditioned on actions.
- Plan in latent space, decode actions.

## Why it matters

Large language models excel at symbolic manipulation but do not inherently maintain grounded state about the physical or interactive world. World-model research asks whether **predictive learning in latent space** is a missing ingredient for agency.

## Limitations

Many demos remain in narrow simulators. Scaling to open-ended environments raises questions about **stochasticity**, partial observability, and whether latent predictors inherit the same data hunger as LLMs.

## My take

World models are best understood as a **research program**, not a single architecture. The useful question is which prediction target (pixels, latents, tokens, energies) buys the most planning power per flop — not whether one paper “solved” AGI.
