## The claim

Frontier AI progress is decoupling from naive scaling: labs still scale, but competitive advantage increasingly depends on data curation, evaluation, inference stacks, and controllable objectives.

## The context

After GPT-4 class models, public discourse still treats parameters and FLOPs as the primary story. Practitioners report a different reality: post-training, tooling, and verification dominate product quality.

## The technical core

- **Data** — High-quality, domain-specific corpora matter more than raw web scale.
- **Objectives** — RLHF, process supervision, and latent prediction explore different capability frontiers.
- **Inference** — Test-time compute and routing change effective intelligence per dollar.
- **Evaluation** — Benchmarks saturate while real tasks remain unsolved.

## The strongest objection

Scaling may still unlock emergent capabilities we cannot anticipate; abandoning it prematurely would forfeit compounding gains.

## What comes next

Expect portfolios of models, world-model modules, and evaluation-first development—not a single ever-larger monolith.
