## The result

Benchmark scores continue to rise while user-reported failure modes persist on deployment tasks.

## The core idea

Evaluation should stress robustness, interaction, and verifiable outcomes—not single-number leaderboard climbs.

## Why it matters

Policy and procurement decisions increasingly cite public benchmarks without understanding construct validity.

## Limitations

Interactive evals are expensive and hard to standardize across labs.

## My take

Treat benchmarks as instruments: useful when you know what they measure, misleading when you do not.
