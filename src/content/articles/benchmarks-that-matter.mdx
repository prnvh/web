## The result

Leaderboard gains often shrink under independent replication, harder prompts, or decontaminated test sets.

## The core idea

A benchmark is a **contract** between researchers and reality. Weak contracts produce pretty charts, not knowledge.

## Why it matters

Policy and procurement read headlines from benchmarks. Misleading evals allocate capital to the wrong problems.

## Limitations

No single suite captures generality. Composite scores hide failure modes.

## My take

Publish **error analyses** alongside averages. Treat +2 points as meaningless until the eval’s sensitivity is documented.
