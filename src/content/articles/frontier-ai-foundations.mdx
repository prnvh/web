## The claim

Most public debate about AI progress confuses **capabilities** with **narratives**. Foundations matter: what was trained, on what data, with how much compute, and against which evals.

## The context

Frontier labs publish selectively. Independent readers need a repeatable lens: ask for training data characteristics, compute budget order-of-magnitude, and whether evals are **held-out** or **contaminated**.

## The simple explanation

Bigger models + more data + more flops generally improve loss curves. That does not automatically map to reliable agency, truthfulness, or economic value — those require different measurements.

## My view

Start every new result with three questions: **What changed in the stack? What is the eval really testing? Who benefits if we believe the headline?**
