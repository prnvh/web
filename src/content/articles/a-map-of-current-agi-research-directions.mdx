## The problem

“AGI” functions as both a **research north star** and a **marketing label**. This map separates camps by what they optimize, what they assume about data, and how they propose to measure generality.

## Major camps / approaches

### Scale + alignment stack

Continue pretraining larger multimodal models; align with RLHF/RLAIF; add tools and memory at the product layer. **Bet:** generality is an emergent property of scale plus interaction data.

### World models & latent prediction

Learn environment dynamics in latent space; combine with planners or LLM interfaces. **Bet:** agency requires predictive state, not just symbolic fluency.

### Neurosymbolic / modular stacks

Explicit reasoning modules, verifiers, or program interpreters wrapped around neural nets. **Bet:** reliability requires structure beyond end-to-end tokens.

### Post-transformer architectures

State-space models, long-conv hybrids, retrieval-heavy layers. **Bet:** attention-dominated stacks hit efficiency or reasoning ceilings.

## Important papers

Use this section as a reading list anchor — add your own notes as the field moves:

- Scaling laws and emergent abilities literature (Kaplan, Hoffmann, Wei et al.)
- World Models (Ha & Schmidhuber)
- JEPA / latent prediction position papers (LeCun et al.)
- ARC-style generalization benchmarks (Chollet)

## Open questions

- What **capabilities** are still missing after tool use and long context?
- Which objectives improve **closed-loop** performance, not leaderboard snapshots?
- Can we measure generality without hidden training-set overlap?

## What seems promising

Hybrid systems: LLM interfaces + verifiers + latent predictors for state. Investment in **evaluation** that rewards multi-step reliability.

## What seems overhyped

Single-number “human-level” claims, demo videos without reproducible evals, and AGI timelines driven by fundraising cycles rather than falsifiable benchmarks.
